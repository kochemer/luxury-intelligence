Optimizing Last-Mile Delivery With AI

Lori Boyer

April 7, 2025

4 min read

For retailers, brands, and ecommerce businesses, last-mile delivery is often the most expensive and unpredictable part of the shipping process. Customers expect fast, reliable deliveries, and a poor last-mile experience can lead to frustration, bad reviews, and lost sales.

The challenge? Choosing the right carrier and service for each package while balancing speed, cost, and reliability. Traditionally, businesses rely on static shipping rules, past experiences, or guesswork to make these decisionsbut AI is changing that.

AI-powered last-mile logistics solutions help businesses analyze carrier performance, automate shipping decisions, and improve delivery outcomesall without the need to manage their own delivery fleets.

Start small with clear objectives and scale gradually. Kushal Pillay

This blog is part of a series that dives into four major ways AI can transform your shipping strategy:

  1. Improving time-in-transit predictions (Read it here)
  2. Transforming the customer experience (Read it here)
  3. Eliminating inefficiencies in the warehouse (Read it here)
  4. Optimizing last-mile delivery

Smarter carrier selection based on performance data

Not all carriers perform equally across all regions, shipment types, or service levels. AI analyzes millions of past deliveries to identify:

  • Which carriers consistently meet on-time delivery promises
  • Which services perform best in specific locations or under certain conditions
  • How carrier performance shifts based on seasonality, weather, and demand surges

Instead of relying on trial and error, retailers can use AI-powered insights to select the best-performing carriers for each shipment.

AI-driven shipping method optimization

Many businesses overpay for shipping because they default to faster, more expensive options, assuming thats whats needed. AI removes the guesswork by automating shipping decisions based on:

  • Cost vs. speed trade-offs (e.g., when ground shipping is just as fast as express)
  • Delivery success rates (ensuring the chosen service is reliable in that region)
  • Real-time carrier pricing and service availability

For example, AI might analyze past shipments and recommend switching 40% of next-day air shipments to ground, saving thousands of dollars without affecting delivery times.

Predictive delivery performance and customer experience

Customers hate vague or inaccurate delivery windows. AI solves this by analyzing real-time shipping conditions and historical data to:

  • Provide more accurate delivery estimates at checkout
  • Proactively detect and alert customers about potential delays
  • Optimize carrier selections based on on-time delivery trends in each region

This not only reduces missed deliveries and customer complaints but also improves trust and satisfaction.

Cost reduction and operational efficiency

By continuously analyzing carrier pricing, delivery speed, and reliability, AI can help businesses:

  • Reduce shipping costs by automatically choosing lower-cost options that still meet customer expectations
  • Improve delivery success rates, reducing returns, re-ships, and lost packages
  • Optimize multi-carrier shipping strategies to prevent bottlenecks and capacity issues

For high-volume shippers, these optimizations can lead to massive cost savings and more predictable logistics operations.

AI is reshaping last-mile deliverynot by optimizing fleet operations, but by helping businesses make smarter carrier and service choices. Instead of guessing which shipping method is best, AI analyzes real data, automates decisions, and continuously improves outcomes.

Now is the time to invest in AI

AI is no longer a luxury in logisticsits a necessity. From predicting delivery times with greater accuracy to enhancing customer service, optimizing warehouse operations, and improving last-mile efficiency, AI is transforming every stage of the supply chain. Companies that embrace AI-driven solutions gain a competitive edge, reduce costs, and improve overall efficiency, while those that hesitate risk falling behind.

The logistics industry is evolving faster than ever, and AI is at the center of this transformation. By leveraging AI-powered tools, businesses can eliminate inefficiencies, enhance decision-making, and future-proof their operations.

The question is no longer if AI will impact logisticsits how fast companies can adopt it to stay ahead.
